How to read each location
Five numbers that together tell you whether a location is on track — and what type of problem it has.
① Passed
% who hit the pass mark
The “can this location move on?” number. Unlike an average, 2 star performers completing 100% can’t make this look good if everyone else is at 0%.
② Never Started
% who haven’t opened anything
These people haven’t touched a single course. They need a reminder or push to get going — very different from someone who started but got stuck halfway.
③ Typical person
What the middle of the team has done
The middle score — not an average. If 2 people are at 100% and 48 are at 5%, the average looks like 9% but the middle score honestly shows 5%.
④ Team spread
How the team is divided
Breaks the team into 4 buckets: not started / barely started / almost there / passed. Shows whether the problem is getting people to start, or getting them to finish.
⑤ Starters who passed
Of those who began, how many finished
If this is high but few people overall passed, you just need to get more people to open their first course. If this is low, people are starting but dropping off.
